Punctuation

All punctuation marks (question marks, exclamation marks, period, comma, semicolon, colon, virgel) are transcribed as printed. The punctuation is not normalized according to today's standards.

Punctuation marks appear directly after the preceding word without spaces. This is followed by a space. Quotation marks and parentheses appear directly next to the text they enclose, without spaces. Footnote references without spaces also follow the preceding character directly.

The impression left on the page by a spatium which has fallen off during the printing process is usually not transcribed.

Several dots in a row, which indicate an omission, are enclosed by a space. An exception to this are dots that indicate an incomplete word. Without spaces, they appear directly after the incomplete word.

There is a space before and after dashes in the middle of the sentence. If the dash precedes a punctuation mark, no space is typed in between.

The Unicode table General Punctuation is valid for punctuation.
